Yahoo Finance Use Case

Title: Browsing Financial News and Data

Description: Cool Dude would likes to check his financial portfolio, check the latest financial news, and explore an investment opportunity.

Name of User and their role: Cool Dude is an engineer who likes to keep tabs on the financial markets, his financial portfolio, and explore investment opportunities.

Usage Preconditions: A Yahoo account is not needed to utilize the website but is preferred in order to save setting and track financial portfolios and/or individual investments.

Usage Post Conditions: Making well informed investment decisions and being up to date on the latest in business news and the broader markets domestically and abroad.

Interaction Flow:

  • Cool Dude enters finance.yahoo.com website into his browser of choice.
  • The home page opens on his monitor.
  • Cool Dude first views all the financial data immediately on his screen.
  • Cool Dude first glances at the broader market indices to get an idea of how the overall domestic markets are performing.
  • Cool Dude then glances at the financial news headlines and quickly reads and glances at the most recent article headlines that from top to bottom on the page.
  • After reviewing the headlines Cool Dude selects an article of interest to read further or moves on to other site options.
  • After browsing the financial headlines Cool Dude then checks his portfolio of stocks to get a general idea how they are performing against the broader market indices.
  • If Cool Dude notices any single investment not inline with the broader market he will select it to understand why.
  • After selecting an individual investment Cool Dude can select a number of options regarding the individual investment, starting with the individual headline news followed by the daily trend chart.
  • If all looks well and not to out of the ordinary Cool Dude can now select other individual investment choices either in his portfolio or to explore other investment options not is his portfolio
  • Cool Dude, having quickly checked the financial news, portfolio, and explored investment opportunities can either move forward with new financial decisions or close his browser and return to work.

Frequency of Use: Several times a day, even weekends, but more during the weekdays when markets are open.
